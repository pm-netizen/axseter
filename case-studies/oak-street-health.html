The Challenge

Oak Street Health was in the midst of aggressive expansion, opening primary care clinics focused on Medicare-eligible patients across the United States. With a growth trajectory that demanded 50+ new clinics per year, their facilities team faced a significant operational challenge: coordinating low-voltage infrastructure installation across dozens of simultaneous construction projects in different markets.

The existing approach—using local contractors in each market—created several problems:

  • Inconsistent quality: Different contractors delivered varying installation quality, leading to operational issues after clinic opening
  • Vendor management burden: The facilities team spent significant time sourcing, vetting, and managing contractors in new markets
  • Schedule risks: Contractor availability varied by market, sometimes causing delays in clinic openings
  • Cost unpredictability: Pricing varied significantly by region, making budgeting difficult
  • Support fragmentation: When issues arose post-opening, there was no clear path to resolution

Oak Street Health needed a partner who could match their expansion velocity while delivering consistent quality nationwide.

The Solution

Axseter became Oak Street Health's Single Point of Contact for low-voltage infrastructure across their new clinic buildouts. The engagement began with a comprehensive standardization effort:

Infrastructure Standardization

Working with Oak Street Health's IT and facilities leadership, we developed standardized deployment specifications for their clinic format. Every location would receive:

  • Structured cabling with defined drop counts by room type
  • Cloud-based access control covering entry points, medication rooms, and staff areas
  • IP video surveillance with standardized camera placements
  • Sound masking in patient areas to support HIPAA compliance

This standardization created a repeatable deployment package that could be executed consistently regardless of location.

Integrated Project Coordination

For each new clinic, Axseter coordinated with Oak Street Health's construction managers and general contractors. We scheduled installations to align with construction milestones, ensuring low-voltage work happened at the optimal point in the build sequence.

A dedicated Axseter project manager served as the single point of contact for all low-voltage matters—eliminating the need for Oak Street Health's team to coordinate multiple vendors.

National Execution

As Oak Street Health opened clinics across 21 states, Axseter's national technician network ensured consistent deployment. Whether the clinic was in Chicago, Dallas, or Providence, the same standards applied and the same quality was delivered.

The Results

Vendor Consolidation

Oak Street Health reduced their low-voltage vendor count by 40%. Instead of managing relationships with dozens of regional contractors, their facilities team worked with one partner who handled execution nationwide.

Consistent Quality

Post-opening infrastructure issues dropped significantly. Standardized specifications and quality verification processes meant clinics opened with properly functioning technology infrastructure.

Schedule Reliability

Low-voltage work stopped being a schedule risk. Axseter's national coverage and proactive coordination ensured installations completed on time, supporting Oak Street Health's aggressive opening calendar.

Simplified Support

When issues arose at any location, one call to Axseter dispatched a technician. No more tracking down which contractor installed which system at which location.

"Axseter's SPoC model eliminated the coordination headaches we had managing regional contractors. One call, one invoice, consistent quality across every new clinic."

Director of Facilities Oak Street Health (CVS Health)

Services Delivered

Structured Cabling

Cat6A cabling throughout each clinic, supporting clinical workstations, EHR systems, and medical devices. Standardized drop counts by room type with consistent labeling and documentation.

Access Control

Cloud-based access control covering main entries, staff entrances, medication storage, and sensitive areas. Integrated with Oak Street Health's credential management system for centralized administration.

Video Surveillance

IP cameras covering entries, parking areas, reception, and pharmacies. Cloud-based video management providing centralized access to all clinic footage.

Sound Masking

Acoustic privacy systems in patient areas, supporting HIPAA compliance by preventing overheard conversations in waiting rooms and check-in areas.

Key Takeaways

The Oak Street Health engagement demonstrates the value of the SPoC model for rapidly growing healthcare organizations:

  • Standardization enables velocity: Defined deployment packages allow rapid execution without sacrificing quality
  • National coverage reduces risk: One partner who can execute anywhere eliminates market-by-market vendor sourcing
  • Consolidated relationships simplify operations: Fewer vendors means less administrative burden and clearer accountability
  • Consistent infrastructure supports consistent care: When technology works reliably, clinical staff can focus on patients
