Midwest Dental Partners

How a regional DSO eliminated infrastructure inconsistencies by switching from GC-managed to owner-direct low voltage installation.

The Challenge

Midwest Dental Partners had grown to 85 locations across the upper Midwest through a combination of de novo builds and acquisitions. Like most DSOs, they let their general contractors handle low voltage as part of the construction package.

The result: 85 locations with 85 different interpretations of "standard" infrastructure.

What GC-Managed Looked Like

  • Different cable labeling conventions at every location
  • Inconsistent rack layouts—IT couldn't deploy equipment remotely
  • No documentation for 40% of locations
  • Network issues required site visits to diagnose
  • Service calls took 3-5 days because nobody knew the original installer

The IT team was spending 60% of their time on infrastructure firefighting instead of supporting clinical operations. Every new location opening was a surprise—would the cabling match their standards or not?

The Turning Point

When Midwest Dental planned their next wave of 15 de novo locations, the VP of IT made the case for carving low voltage out of the GC scope. The construction team was skeptical—wouldn't this create coordination headaches?

They ran a pilot: five locations with Axseter as an owner-direct contractor, five locations GC-managed as usual.

The Results

Owner-Direct Outcomes

  • 100% documentation compliance on day one
  • Identical rack layouts—IT could deploy equipment without site visits
  • Same-day service response through ongoing relationship
  • Predictable pricing—no surprise change orders
  • Zero network issues at go-live across all five locations

The GC-managed locations? Two had documentation. One had a cable labeling scheme that matched their standard. The IT team spent the first two weeks at each location re-documenting and remediating.

The pilot made the decision obvious. Midwest Dental moved all new construction to owner-direct and began a remediation program for existing locations.

Three Years Later

Midwest Dental has grown to 127 locations. The IT team now spends their time on clinical technology initiatives instead of infrastructure triage. New locations open with predictable, standardized infrastructure every time.

"We got our IT team back. They're supporting dentists now instead of chasing cable documentation and troubleshooting inconsistent infrastructure."

— VP of Information Technology, Midwest Dental Partners
