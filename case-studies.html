Case Studies

See how Axseter has helped multi-location enterprises streamline their low voltage infrastructure with consistent, documented deployments.

From healthcare systems expanding through acquisition to insurance brokerages integrating hundreds of acquired agencies, these case studies illustrate how national low voltage partnerships solve the infrastructure challenges that come with rapid multi-location growth. Each engagement demonstrates our commitment to consistent quality, comprehensive documentation, and responsive support across every market we serve.

Healthcare

Oak Street Health / CVS Health

Oak Street Health, now part of CVS Health, is one of the nation's fastest-growing medical retail providers—opening dozens of new locations annually. Before partnering with Axseter, they relied on general contractors to independently select low voltage vendors for each location, resulting in inconsistent installation quality, unclear accountability, and repair timelines that stretched into weeks.

Axseter implemented a process-driven approach: floorplan discrepancy reports that catch issues before construction, structured GC kickoff calls, weekly coordination throughout the build, and live IT punch walks via video. We became their single point of accountability for all low voltage infrastructure—eliminating finger-pointing and accelerating problem resolution from weeks to hours.

"

For fast-growing organizations like Oak Street Health and CVS, consistency and speed are everything. By embedding ourselves into the construction and IT processes, we've saved time, eliminated waste, and ensured every new location opens with confidence.

Healthcare

ATI Physical Therapy

ATI Physical Therapy operates 850+ clinics across 24 states—one of the largest single-branded physical therapy networks in the country. With that kind of footprint, they need a low voltage partner who can execute consistently whether the clinic is in Chicago, Phoenix, or anywhere in between.

Over the past several years, Axseter has completed hundreds of clinic turn-ups for ATI. The scope is straightforward—structured cabling, access control, and TV installations—but the value is in the consistency. Same standards, same quality, same process at every location. No surprises, no variations, no scrambling for a local vendor.

"

Hundreds of locations. One vendor. Consistent execution every time.

Financial Services

Arthur J. Gallagher & Co.

Arthur J. Gallagher, the world's third-largest insurance broker, completed its $13.5 billion acquisition of AssuredPartners in August 2025—adding 250+ office locations across 38 states to its existing footprint. Each acquired location comes with its own legacy infrastructure: different cabling standards, access control systems, and IT configurations installed by dozens of regional contractors over the years.

For companies executing aggressive M&A strategies, Axseter provides the infrastructure standardization backbone. One partner to assess, remediate, and bring acquired locations up to corporate standards—across structured cabling, surveillance, and access control. No need to coordinate dozens of regional vendors. One contract, one point of accountability, one standard.

"

When you're integrating hundreds of acquired locations, you need a partner who can execute consistently at national scale. That's exactly what Axseter delivers.
